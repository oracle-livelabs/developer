Q: What role does chunking do?
- It's a hidden function that creates fresh, chunky dog food ondemand
- Encrypts sensitive customer data
* Splits text into smaller chunks to generate vector embeddings used with vector indexes or hybrid vector indexes.
- Creates backup copies of database tables in bite-sized chunks
> Chunking is a critical mechanism as embedding models have fixed token limits and, more importantly, smaller, semantically focused chunks produce more precise embeddings that match what a user is searching for rather than burying the relevant signal in a sea of unrelated context.

Q: What is vector quantization?
- It's what happens when your SatNav can't decide between two routes and just picks a third one at random
* When high-dimensional vectors are compressed by mapping them to a smaller set of representative codewords
- The process by which quantum physicists determine exactly how many vectors can fit in a lab coat pocket before Schrödinger's cat knocks them all off the table
- A government program that reduces the number of opinions allowed in a meeting by clustering similar ones together
> With vector quantization, you get "compression," but are trading slight precision loss for faster search and reduced storage.
